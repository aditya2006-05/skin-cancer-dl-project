Overview

Abstract

A high-level overview of the research proposal and its clinical significance

🎯

Research Goal

Develop a dual-stage Deep Learning framework to enhance skin cancer detection by combining precise image segmentation (U-Net) with high-accuracy classification (EfficientNet / ViT).

⚡

Core Innovation

Automate the screening of malignancies such as Melanoma and Basal Cell Carcinoma through pixel-wise boundary detection feeding into transfer-learning classifiers.

⚠️

Key Challenges

Dataset bias toward lighter skin tones, severe class imbalance, and lack of transparency in "black-box" neural networks hinder clinical adoption.

🔬

Future Direction

Transition toward Explainable AI (XAI), fusion of image + patient metadata, and lightweight model optimization for mobile teledermatology.

About the Dataset

The HAM10000 ("Human Against Machine") dataset is a large collection of multi-source dermatoscopic images of common pigmented skin lesions. Ground truth verification was performed through histopathology, expert consensus, and in-vivo confocal microscopy.

  • Total Images: 10,015
  • Unique Lesions: 7,470
  • Disease Classes: 7
  • Metadata Fields: Age, Sex, Localization
  • Ground Truth: Histopathology + Expert Consensus

U

U-Net Architecture

  • Encoder-decoder with skip connections
  • Preserves spatial resolution
  • Handles small lesion boundaries
  • Trained on ISIC segmentation masks
  • Output: Per-pixel binary segmentation mask
E

EfficientNet / MobileNetV2

  • Pre-trained on ImageNet (transfer learning)
  • Fine-tuned on HAM10000 lesion crops
  • Handles class imbalance via weighted loss
  • MobileNetV2 optimized for edge/mobile
  • Output: Softmax over 7 disease classes
V

Vision Transformer (ViT)

  • Patch-based attention mechanism
  • Captures long-range feature dependencies
  • Outperforms CNNs on large datasets
  • Attention maps align with clinical findings
  • Output: Class token classification head

Goals

Project Objectives

What we aim to achieve through this research

01

Automated Framework

Develop a fully automated, end-to-end deep learning system that requires no manual feature engineering or clinical intervention for initial screening.

02

Equitable Performance

Ensure reliable diagnostic accuracy across diverse patient populations, addressing dataset bias toward lighter skin tones through data augmentation and balanced sampling.

03

Clinical Interpretability (XAI)

Integrate Grad-CAM and SHAP methods to generate visual explanations, bridging the "black-box" gap and building clinician trust in AI-generated predictions.

04

Diagnostic Accuracy

Exceed traditional CAD system baselines by achieving >90% accuracy and >0.90 AUC-ROC on the HAM10000 benchmark dataset.

05

Teledermatology Deployment

Optimize lightweight model variants (MobileNetV2) for mobile deployment, enabling accessible early-stage screening in regions with limited dermatology specialists.
